Frequently Asked Questions

IEPF (Investor Education and Protection Fund) is a government fund where shares and dividends are transferred when dividends remain unclaimed for 7 consecutive years. This is done under the Companies Act, 2013 to protect investor interests.

The IEPF claim process typically takes 3-6 months from the date of filing, depending on the complexity of the case and the responsiveness of the concerned authorities.

Typically you need: Aadhar card, PAN card, original share certificates (if available), proof of ownership, bank details, and a demat account. In case of transmission (inheritance), you'll also need a death certificate, succession certificate, and legal heir documents.

We offer a free initial consultation to assess your case. Our fee structure is discussed transparently during the consultation. Contact us to know more about our charges.

Yes, NRIs can claim shares from IEPF. The process requires additional documentation like passport copies, overseas address proof, and NRE/NRO bank account details. We regularly handle NRI claims and can guide you through the process.
